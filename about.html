Community Services.net

We bridge the gap between practice and evidence.

Our journey →

CSnet was created in 2011 by Mandy Doon and Tim Gleeson, driven by a shared belief that meaningful data should help demonstrate real, lasting change in people's lives.

Working in the community service sector, they saw first-hand the gap between everyday practice and the evidence organisations were expected to produce. CSnet was developed to bridge that gap.

More than case management software, CSnet tells one connected story across the sector and your programs — revealing patterns, supporting strategic decision-making, and inviting organisations into place-based communities of practice.

Our mission is to support your theory of change. We're proudly built by people who've worked in the sector, for teams and organisations doing the work that matters most.

"Real change happens when compassion, strong partnerships and evidence combine."

Our journey

From a shared belief to a sector-wide platform.

CSnet has grown steadily — expanding its reach, refining its technology, and strengthening its impact alongside the organisations it serves.

2011
Foundation

Formal company formation

Community Services.net Pty Ltd took over operations of CSnet®, bringing new governance, structure and capacity to scale the service. A platform built from the ground up for the community sector — by people who knew it from the inside.

2015
B Corp

Becoming a Certified B Corp

Achieving B Corp certification in February 2015 was an important moment in defining CSnet's identity — recognising our commitment to high standards of social and environmental performance, accountability, and transparency. Being a B Corp ensures our values remain embedded in our DNA as we grow.

2016
Partnership

Partnership with DFFH and IRIS integration

CSnet begins formal collaboration with the Victorian Department of Families, Fairness and Housing to support IRIS data migration and reporting integration — enabling organisations to manage IRIS and non-IRIS programs within a single CSnet database.

Today
Continuing expansion

A platform that grows with the sector

CSnet operates from offices in Brisbane and Melbourne, supporting services in communities across Australia. We work closely with partners to improve outcomes and embed evidence-based practice — remaining committed to systemic change through accurate, respectful and real-time information.

Case study

Strong in Family and Culture

Central Queensland Indigenous Development (CQID) is an Aboriginal Community Controlled Organisation supporting Aboriginal and Torres Strait Islander children, families and communities across Central Queensland.

Through its Strong in Family and Culture program, CQID works to keep children connected to family, culture, community and identity — responding to the over-representation of Aboriginal children in the child protection system.

Since 2021, CSnet has worked alongside CQID to build a system that reflects how CQID works, speaks and understands impact — protecting sensitive family storylines, strengthening cultural practice, and turning everyday work into evidence for learning.

Read the full story →
🌳
Protecting family stories

CQID mapped around 200 family members for one young person — reaching back five or six generations. CSnet holds that information with care, respect and confidence.

📊
Turning practice into evidence

By capturing family-finding strategies in CSnet, CQID can see patterns over time, understand what works, and share learning across the sector.

🤝
A partnership built on trust

No existing model to follow. The system evolved as the program developed — walking alongside CQID in the direction they were already travelling.
